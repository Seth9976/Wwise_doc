Removing DC offsets

The DC offset represents the percentage away from zero amplitude that a signal lies. The DC offset can cause problems for sounds. You can remove the offset, but if you choose to keep it, be aware of the following undesirable effects in Wwise:

  • A normalized sound with a DC offset does not reach its highest volume during audio processing because the offset consumes the headroom. This problem can extend to the mix as a whole because mixing two sounds where one is with a DC offset and the other isn't causes them both to have a DC offset.

  • Any signals with recursive DSP algorithms applied are likely to be sensitive to audio files with DC offsets and will cause artifacts. This is the case particularly for reverb plug-ins and Environmental FX plug-ins.

Caution

We recommend not removing the DC offset for looping sounds. The remove mechanism is a high-pass filter, so there is no guarantee that it will modify the first and last sample of the loop in the same way because it does not know they will play contiguously. This can cause a discontinuity in the signal, which you might hear as a click.

To avoid performance issues, Wwise doesn't remove the DC offset at run-time. Consequently, it is recommended to remove the DC offset before importing files, either with a filter in your audio authoring tool or during the Wwise conversion process in the Conversion Settings Editor.
